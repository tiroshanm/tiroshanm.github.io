About

Research & Vision

My research journey centers on advancing the intersection of artificial intelligence and knowledge representation. I am particularly fascinated by how machines can better understand, reason with, and generate knowledge through sophisticated graph structures and semantic understanding.

At the National Institute of Informatics in Japan, I work on developing novel approaches for knowledge graph embeddings, focusing on negative sampling strategies that improve representation learning. My doctoral research introduced innovative methods like MDNCaching and TuckerDNCaching.

As a lecturer at the University of Kelaniya, I am committed to nurturing the next generation of computer scientists, sharing my passion for programming, software engineering, and cloud technologies.
